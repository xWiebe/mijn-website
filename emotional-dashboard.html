Emotional Dashboard

"Emotional Dashboard" is part of a larger project called: "Dynamic Identities" by Fontys InnovationLab.

Dynamic Identities: what if these could talk?

Imagine a future where buildings are intelligent and interactive. Students of Fontys Hogeschool ICT designed and developed interactive installations that investigate the interaction between people and their school building.

More about the project

My contribution to Dynamic Identities: Emotional Dashboard

I participated to this project through school. Multiple students were invited to collaborate with Hyperculture and StudioKrom. The goal of this project was to create an interactive installation to expose during Dutch Design Week 2022. The overarching concept of the project was emotions. I individually built my own part of the interactive installation called "Emotional Dashboard".

Emotional Dashboard is a real-time dashboard of emotions. The installation works with a camera that observes the user's facial expression. When it has determined the facial expression of the user, the installation will visualise random squares in different colours. The colours visualised in the installation are based on the emotions that are known to the system.

The process that lead to the creation of Emotional Dashboard

This section of the page is about the process that lead me to the creation of Emotional Dashboard. I participated in the project "Dynamic Identities" from Fontys InnovationLab as a school project. The assignment given to us (students) was to create a big interactive installation based on emotions. The first step in this assignment was thinking about what we wanted to make. To start, I created a few different ideas to get inspired.

1st Idea: Emotional Lines

Since the stakeholders of the project mentioned that it would be interesting to think about how the building looks at us I started thinking about it. I thought about it allot to get an idea of how the building would see us. The way I see it is that the building is always there. It gets to see allot of different people and creates an opinion based on what it sees.

There is a beamer and a camera on the ceiling. The camera has multiple purposes. The first purpose of the camera is to detect the location of the actors entering the floor. The second purpose of the camera is to detect the emotion of the actors. With the beamer, lines will be projected on the ground in the colour linked to the actor's emotion. The path the actors walk will be used as real-time reference for the lines to be drawn on the ground.

2nd Idea: Stair of Emotions

Another idea I had was the Stair of Emotions. To simply explain this idea; you have multiple screens in the staircase. Each of these screens represent a different emotion. Every screen has a different 3d environment that matches the emotion linked to it. As an example: the angry screen could have a very chaotic volcanic environment (with a staircase of course). There will be a camera pointed at the stairs that looks at the actors from the front. With this camera you would measure the relative bone rotations and the absolute position of the actor. With this data you can animate a 3d character on the screens. The screen where a representation of the actor will be shown can be decided by the actor's facial expression.

3rd Idea: Emotional Dashboard

The last idea I had was to create a dashboard that shows the emotions inside TQ. I really like the concept of this idea but I had no idea how I would visualise it.

The first Idea about visualising this concept was as followed. An image would be shown with allot of faces on it, all the faces would be 3d models that could be changed in real-time based on the facial expressions of the actors.

What I didn't like about this visualisation was the absolute representation of the emotions. Because of the absoluteness of this idea there was no space for people to interpret their own idea to it. This is the reason why I started thinking about a new way to visualise my concept in a more abstract way

Another way of visualising the emotions inside TQ was to create an individual bubble for each actor entering the building. The colour of the bubble would be based on the facial expression of the actor.

What I liked about this idea is that it's an abstract way of showing the emotions inside the building, by linking colours to different emotions I could create a dashboard based on the facial expressions of the actors. This way people could still interpret the installation in their own way. What I didn't like about this was the way the concept is visualised. The visualisation itsself is too simple. How can I visualise the same concept in a more visually attractive way? I started sketching to see what would visually work and what would not.

After sketching allot of different visualisations I came to the conclusion that the visualisation would become more interesting the more complicated it becomes. The next step was to realise this concept in code. To make the coding easier I decided to work with squares at first. This way I could prototype my concept fast.

After creating this prototype I came to the conclusion that working with squares would make it visually interesting enough to be satisfied with what I have build. I added a library that would link an emotion to the facial expression of the actor. For each emotion I linked the colour that was given for this assigment by Hyperspace.
